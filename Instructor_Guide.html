My Trip, My Book

Instructor Guide — a photo trip-book class for seniors, 4 steps (adaptable to 3)

This is the facilitator copy. It has everything you need to run the class — setup, step scripts with teacher notes, and checklists. Hand students the separate Student Handbook: same steps, plain language, no notes.

The goal

By the end, each student has a trip book that:

  1. Flips like a real book — tap or click to turn the pages.
  2. Lives in Claude — they add to it by uploading photos from any phone or laptop, and can download the finished book any time.

There is no separate app or tool to install. The student talks to Claude, uploads photos with the + button, and Claude builds and updates the book directly.

How it works — the whole idea

  1. Student gets their own Claude Project, set up once in Step 1 by a helper.
    This is where the book-building rules live — the student never sees or touches them.
  2. Inside that project, the student starts a chat and clicks + to upload photos (phone, tablet, or computer — anywhere).
  3. Claude adds each photo as a new page in the flippable trip book.
  4. Claude shows each photo in turn and asks about it; the student gives a line by typing or talking. (Optional.)
  5. The book updates on screen. The student can flip through it and download it any time.

Because uploading uses the chat +, students can add photos between steps, from home, from their phone — they just reopen their project and continue.

What you'll use

  • Claude (claude.ai), open in a web browser — one Project per student.
  • trip_book_spec.md — the build spec, attached once to each student's project Context in Step 1.
  • The "Start my book" line below, to begin each student's book.
  • San_Francisco_demo_book.html — a finished example to show the goal on day one.

Who and how

  • Group size: 6–10 students. Aim for 1 helper per 3–4 students.
  • Step length: 90 minutes with a short break.
  • Devices: a laptop, tablet, or phone per student — anything that opens Claude in a browser.
  • Room: a projector for demos; good lighting; water.
The setup steps: students create their own Claude account and Project — this is a real, if short, learning task, so budget 10–15 minutes per student and have helpers ready to sit with anyone who gets stuck, especially at phone verification (below).

Setting up each student's project

Students do this themselves, with a helper alongside. Do this before photos go in — either as a pre-step slot or as the start of Step 1.

Part 1 — Create a Claude account

  1. Open a browser and go to claude.ai.
  2. Click Sign up. Choose "Continue with Google" (fastest, if they have a Google/Gmail account) or "Continue with email".
  3. If using email: enter the email address, then check that inbox for a message from Claude and click the verification link (or enter the code shown).
  4. Enter a phone number and request a code.
    This step trips people up — read carefully. Anthropic requires phone verification for every new account, with no way to skip it. It must be a real mobile number that can receive text messages. Landlines, Google Voice, and other app-generated numbers are rejected. A personal mobile phone is the safest bet.
  5. Enter the 6-digit code that arrives by text message within a minute or two.
  6. Once the Claude chat screen appears, the account is ready.

Part 2 — Create a Project

  1. Click Projects in the left-hand menu (or go to claude.ai/projects).
  2. Click + New Project.
  3. Name it after the trip (e.g. "Italy 2019") and confirm.

Part 3 — Attach the spec file

  1. Inside the project, find the Context section (right side of the project page) and click + to add content.
  2. Choose trip_book_spec.md from wherever it was saved on the student's device.
    Students need this file on their own device before this step — it doesn't come with their account. Email it to every student ahead of Step 1 (have them save the attachment to Downloads or Photos), or hand it out in class via a shared link or USB stick.

Start my book — paste this into the chat

"Please start my flip-through trip book. The title is [trip name] and the dates are [dates]. I'll upload all my photos with the + button now."

This is pasted once the project and spec file are set up — see the Step 1 script below. After this, students just upload photos — the rules already attached to the project handle the rest (adding pages, asking about each photo, skip logic, facts, sizing.)

Before the first class — note to students

Bring your photos on your phone (or wherever they are). Pick one trip, and think of a title and the dates. You do not need to move the photos anywhere first — we'll upload them straight from your phone in class. You'll also need a mobile phone that can receive text messages, even if you're working on a laptop — it's used once, to verify your new account.

The four steps

Each numbered step is a plain instruction to say to the student. Say one, wait until they've done it, then say the next. The shaded notes below steps are for you — don't read them aloud.

Step 1 — Start your book and add all your photos

  1. Open Claude in the browser and create the student's account and Project together — see "Setting up each student's project" above. (Budget 10–15 minutes; have the spec file ready to hand over.)
  2. Watch the example book on the big screen. (Flip through the San Francisco demo.)
  3. Open the Project and start a new chat. Paste the Start my book line with the trip name and dates. Press Enter. (Helper pastes it.)
  4. Click the + at the bottom of the chat.
  5. Choose 4–6 photos to start with. (Hold Ctrl and click on 4–6, or tap each one on a phone. Steer them away from "Select All" — on a phone that can mean their whole camera roll. A handful is plenty to see the book take shape; more can go in afterward.)
  6. Send them. Wait — each photo becomes a page in your book.
  7. Need more? Click + again and add the rest.
  8. Flip through your book on the screen.
Don't add words yet — that's next step. If a phone won't upload, open Claude in the phone's own browser and use + there. The account, project, and spec file are only set up once — Steps 2–4 just reopen the same project.

Step 2 — Add your words (Claude asks you)

  1. Open Claude, open your Project, and continue your chat.
  2. Claude will go through your photos one at a time — showing you each photo and asking about it.
  3. For the photo on screen, tell Claude the place and a line about it — type it, or use your device's microphone to talk.
  4. Not sure what to say? Ask Claude: "Give me a question to help me remember this one."
  5. Don't want words on a photo? Just say "skip." (Claude will name the place and add a short description and a fact for you.)
  6. Flip through and read your book.
All words are optional — a photo-only book is fine. For skipped photos, Claude figures out the place and adds a line plus a fact about any well-known landmark. Students can also add more photos from home anytime with the + button.

Step 3 — Put it in order and polish

  1. Open Claude, open your Project, and continue your chat.
  2. To change the order, tell Claude: "Move the bridge photo to the front."
  3. To take one out, tell Claude: "Remove the blurry photo on page 4."
  4. Want a fact on a page? Ask Claude: "Add a fact about the Cliffs of Moher."
  5. See a small "confirm venue" tag on a photo? Claude wasn't sure of the spot — tell it the real place and it'll update the page: "That's actually Fisherman's Wharf."
  6. Fix any wording: "Change the line on the castle photo to…"
  7. Flip through and check it reads the way you want.
Once every photo in the book has a confirmed location, the closing page automatically gets a small route map — one more reason to clear any "confirm venue" tags.

Step 4 — Download and share it

  1. Open Claude, open your Project, and continue your chat.
  2. Flip through your finished book one more time.
  3. To keep your own copy: click Download on the book. (It saves as one file with all the photos inside.)
  4. To send it to family, pick one: tell Claude "Give me a link I can share" (you publish it, then send the link), or attach the downloaded file to an email.
  5. Show the class your cover and one favourite page.
Students download nothing unless they want a copy of their own. You can publish each book as a link to share with family.

Running this as 3 steps

  • Step 1 — Start the book and upload all your photos.
  • Step 2 — Add your words (Claude asks about each photo) and put them in order.
  • Step 3 — Download and share.

Words to help students remember (give out in Steps 1 & 3)

Students don't need fancy words. One true sentence is plenty. Finish one of these for a photo:

  • "This was the moment we…"
  • "I'll always remember the…"
  • "We didn't expect…"
  • "The best part of this day was…"
  • "We laughed because…"
  • "This is where we…"

Or students can just ask Claude: "Give me a question about this photo."

Facilitator checklist

Before each step

  • (Before Step 1) trip_book_spec.md emailed to every student, or ready on a shared link / USB stick
  • (Before Step 1) Students reminded to bring a mobile phone that can receive text messages, even if working on a laptop
  • The example book ready on the projector
  • The "Start my book" line ready to paste
  • Handouts printed in large type
  • Helpers briefed: guide, don't take over

Watch for

  • Phone verification stalls — wrong number type (landline, Google Voice, VoIP), or the code takes a few minutes to arrive
  • Students who don't have the spec file saved on their device yet when they reach "attach to Context"
  • Trouble uploading photos from a phone — open Claude in the phone's own browser and use + there
  • A "confirm venue" tag left on a page — remind the student to just tell Claude the real place
  • Quiet students who are stuck — circulate and check screens
  • Remind students they can add photos from home anytime, by reopening their project
