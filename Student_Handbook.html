Your handbook — print large, yours to keep and write on.

You are going to make a little book about one of your trips — your photos, in the order you choose, with a few of your own words next to each one. You'll be able to flip through it like a real book, and send it to your family.

No special app to install. If you've ever put photos in an album or a shoebox, you already have every skill you need.

If anything goes too fast, say so. That's not you being difficult — that's you helping. A helper is right there with you.

Getting set up

Before you start your book, you'll create your own free Claude account and a Project to keep it in. Your helper will sit with you — take it one step at a time.

1. Create your account

  1. Go to claude.ai in your browser.
  2. Click Sign up.
  3. Choose "Continue with Google" if you have a Gmail address — it's the quickest way. Otherwise choose "Continue with email" and type your email address.
  4. If you used email: check your inbox for a message from Claude and click the link inside it (or type in the code it shows you).
  5. Type in your mobile phone number when asked, and click to get a code.
  6. A text message will arrive with a 6-digit code. Type that code in.
The phone number step is required for everyone — it's how Claude checks you're a real person. It needs to be a mobile number that can receive text messages (not a home phone). This only happens once.

2. Create your Project

  1. Look for Projects in the menu and click it.
  2. Click + New Project.
  3. Type a name for your trip (like "Italy 2019") and confirm.

3. Add the rules file

  1. Your helper will give you a small file called trip_book_spec.md — by email, a link, or a USB stick. Save it somewhere on your device you can find again, like Downloads.
  2. Inside your new Project, find the Context area and click +.
  3. Choose the trip_book_spec.md file you saved.

That's it — your project now knows how to build your book. You won't need to touch this file again.

The simple loop

This is the whole thing. Once you know this, you know the class.

  1. Open Claude in your browser and open your Project.
  2. Continue your chat inside it.
  3. Click + at the bottom of the chat.
  4. Choose a photo. Claude adds it as a page.
  5. Tell Claude a line to go with it — type or talk. (Optional.)
  6. Repeat for each photo.
  7. Flip through your book any time.
  8. Click Download to keep a copy.

You can add photos from anywhere — even your phone at home. Just open your project and click +.

Step 1 — Start your book and add your photos

  1. Open Claude in your browser and go to your Project. (Already set up? Skip to step 2.)
  2. Watch the example book on the big screen.
  3. Tell Claude the name of your trip and the dates.
  4. Click the + at the bottom of the chat.
  5. Choose 4–6 photos to start with. (On a phone, tap each one — no need to grab your whole camera roll.)
  6. Send them. Watch each one become a page.
  7. Flip through your book on the screen.
Don't add words yet — that's next step.

Step 2 — Add your words

  1. Open Claude, open your project, and continue your chat.
  2. Claude will show you each photo, one at a time, and ask about it.
  3. Tell Claude the place and a line about it — type it, or talk.
  4. Not sure what to say? Ask Claude: "Give me a question to help me remember this one."
  5. Don't want words on a photo? Just say "skip." Claude will add a short description for you.
  6. Flip through and read your book.
Words are optional — a photo-only book is fine too.

Step 3 — Put it in order and polish

  1. Open Claude, open your project, and continue your chat.
  2. To change the order: "Move the bridge photo to the front."
  3. To take one out: "Remove the blurry photo on page 4."
  4. Want a fact on a page? Ask: "Add a fact about the Cliffs of Moher."
  5. See a little "confirm venue" tag on a photo? Claude guessed the spot — just tell it the real place: "That's actually Fisherman's Wharf."
  6. To fix wording: "Change the line on the castle photo to…"
  7. Flip through and check it reads the way you want.
Once every photo's spot is confirmed, your closing page gets a bonus map of your whole route.

Step 4 — Download and share it

  1. Open Claude, open your project, and continue your chat.
  2. Flip through your finished book one more time.
  3. Click Download to keep your own copy. It saves as one file, photos inside.
  4. To send it to family: ask Claude for a share link, or attach the downloaded file to an email.
  5. Show the class your cover and one favourite page!

Words to help you remember

You don't need fancy words. One true sentence is plenty. Finish one of these for a photo:

  • "This was the moment we…"
  • "I'll always remember the…"
  • "We didn't expect…"
  • "The best part of this day was…"
  • "We laughed because…"
  • "This is where we…"

Or just ask Claude: "Give me a question about this photo."

How to share your book

Your book lives in Claude. You can:

  • Flip through it any time in your chat.
  • Download it as one file (photos inside) to keep or email.
  • Get a share link — ask your instructor to publish it, then send the link to family.

To email the file: start an email, attach the file, send. The other person opens it to flip through.

If your email won't let the file through, send the share link instead, or copy the file onto a memory stick.

A few things to remember

  • Always open the same project — that's where your book lives.
  • You can add photos from home, on your phone, any time.
  • Nothing is permanent until you say so. Ask Claude to change anything.
  • This book is yours.
